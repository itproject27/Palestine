The Palestnian Flag

The flag of Palestine is a tricolor of three equal horizontal stripes (black, white, and green from top to bottom) overlaid by a red triangle issuing from the hoist. This flag is derived from the Pan-Arab colors and is used to represent the State of Palestine and the Palestinian people. It was first adopted on 28 May 1964 by the Palestinian Liberation Organization. The flag day is celebrated on 30 September.

Dabke dance

Dabke is an Arab folk dance native to the Levant countries. It is popular in Palestinian culture, and many troupes perform the dance throughout the world. The Dabke is marked by synchronized jumping, stamping, and movement, similar to tap dancing.

Olive Trees

Olive trees are the main agricultural crops in the Palestinian Territories, where they are mostly grown to produce olive oil. It is estimated that olive production represents 57% of the cultivated land in the occupied Palestinian territories, where there were 7.8 million fruit-bearing olive trees in 2011. In 2014, an estimated 108,000 tons of olives were pressed, producing 24,700 tons of olive oil, which contributed $10,900,000. There are about 100,000 families that depend on olives as a basic income.

Palestinian scarf

The Palestinian scarf is a black and white scarf usually worn around the neck or with an agal on the head. The Palestinian keffiyeh has become a Palestinian national symbol, and its use has transcended the Arab region and gained popularity among activists in solidarity with the Palestinians in the conflict with Israel, and among activists of the international left in general.

Al-Aqsa Mosque

Al-Aqsa Mosque is one of the largest mosques in the world and one of the three mosques that Muslims travel to, as the Prophet of Islam Muhammad said. It is also the first of the two qiblahs in Islam. It is located inside the Old City of Jerusalem in Palestine. It is the entire area surrounded by the wall and the name of everything that is inside the wall of Al-Aqsa Mosque, located in the far southeastern corner of the walled Old City.

Maqlupa

Maqluba is considered one of the most famous dishes in the Levant in general and Palestine in particular. People cook it everywhere in Palestine. It dates back to the thirteenth century AD, and it can be cooked using meat or chicken according to availability and also according to desire.

Alqiama Church

The Church of the Holy Sepulchre, or the Church of the Holy Sepulcher, is a church within the walls of the Old City of Jerusalem. The church was built over Golgotha or Golgotha, the site of the rock on which Jesus is believed to have been crucified. It is considered one of the holiest Christian churches, and the most important in the Christian world. According to Christian beliefs, the church contains the place where Jesus was buried, called the Holy Sepulcher.
